Early Life & Biography

Angela was born to a mixed family as her father, Eddie Rye, is of the African-American ethnicity while her mother, Andre Rye, is of the Caucasian ethnicity. She was born in Seattle, Washington, and grew up in its section of Madrona. She has a brother by the name of Brian Rye, and her father is a Seattle community activist.

She started her schooling at the Holy Names Academy, an all-girls school in Seattle, and after graduating, she enrolled at the University of Washington.  She continued with her studies by joining a law school by the name of Seattle University School of Law from where she graduated in 2005.

Career

The career of Rye started with her being an advocate at an association that had 120 black universities and colleges in the United States of America. The association went by the name of the National Association for Equal Opportunity in Higher Education. Angela was the Coordinator of Advocacy and Legislative Affairs.

Before she got the post, she used to work for Congresswoman Maxine Waters in her district office. She was also Director of the Western Region of the National Black Law Students Association.

Angela moved to Washington, DC, upon which she founded IMPACT.  This is an organization that seeks to encourage young professionals in political involvement, economic empowerment as well as civic engagement. Within a short time, the organization has grown to be a powerful voice and advocate for young professionals.

Under Rye’s leadership, IMPACT has partnerships with the National Urban League, National Bar Association, Rainbow/Push, Congressional Black Caucus Foundation, and Black Leadership Forum, among others. She founded IMPACT Strategies in 2013, which is a political advocacy firm, and she is currently the CEO as well as the Principal of the firm.

Angela is currently the only recurring guest on the famous radio station by the name of The Breakfast Club. She made an appearance on the station after Twitter users started championing her name. Her first appearance was in January 2017, after which she has made frequent appearances. She mainly discusses updates on the Trump administration, the policy changes as well as other pop culture issues.

Currently, Angela Rye is a political commentator on CNN. She has also been featured as an on-air personality on many other media outlets, including TV One, BET, HuffPost Live, and CNN.
