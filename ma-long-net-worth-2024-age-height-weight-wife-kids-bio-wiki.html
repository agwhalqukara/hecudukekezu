Early Life & Biography

Ma Long was born on October 20, 1998, in Anshan, Liaoning Province of China. Long started his journey from a tender age of 5 years. The reason behind it was that his parents thought that an injury-free non-contact sport like table tennis was good for his health. His former coaches were Shi Haimei, Bai Xiaodong, and Jiang Wenkun. Long reportedly still keeps in touch with his childhood coaches.

At the age of 10, Ma Long represented his primary school and won the boy’s single and team event in the “baby cup.” Ma Long hasn’t looked back ever since. He earned a place for himself on himself “Liaoning Provincial Team” at the age of 11. Beijing scouted Ma Long when he was 13, and the groundwork to his astonishing career started. In 2003, Ma Long joined the Chinese National B team. By the age of 15, he was selected for the Chinese national A team.

Career

Ma Long won the World Junior Championships in Kobe in 2004. At the age of 17, he became the “youngest-ever world senior champion.” He found a spot in China’s Men’s team, and that same team won the 2006 World Team Championships in Bremen.

In January 2016, he won the German Open (Grand Slam), going undefeated to the final where he beat Vladimir Samsonov 4-1. Due to his No. 1 ranking, he automatically qualified in singles to the third round at the Olympics in Rio. He went on to win the Gold Medal, thus living up to his reputation.

In 2017, he started the year by winning the Qatar Open in February for the second time in a row. In between his brilliant stretches of wins, he also suffered quite a few injuries. His ranking dropped to 9th due to non-participation because of injuries in 2018.

Ma’s injuries persisted in 2019, preventing him from taking part in Marvellous 12, the 2019 WTTC Chinese qualifier in Budapest. However, he came back more reliable, and after defeating Lin Gaoyaun, he posed in front of the camera, appearing to wipe the dust off his racket to indicate he was fine even after being inactive for so long.
