Jasmine Guy

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Tuesday, September 24, 2024

Jasmine Guy is an accomplished and well-known actor, director, singer, and dancer. The guy is best known for her roles as Dina in the 1988 film School Daze and Whitley Gilbert-Wayne in the 1987-1993 NBC The Cosby Show spin-off A Different World.

Guy won six consecutive NAACP Image Awards for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Comedy Series from 1990 to 1995 for her role on the show. She is known as Kayla Samuels on The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air and Roxy Harvey on Dead Like Me.

Where was Jasmine Guy born? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

Jasmine Guy was born to an African-American father and a Portuguese-American mother in Boston, Massachusetts. She grew up in Atlanta, Georgia, in the affluent historic Collier Heights neighborhood, where she attended Northside liberal arts high school, which was later renamed North Atlanta high school.

Her mother, Jaye Rudolph (born 1930), was a former high-school teacher, and her father, Reverend William Guy (born 1928), was the pastor of the historic Friendship Baptists of Atlanta, which served as an early home for Spelman College; he also taught philosophy and religion at the university level. When she was 17, she moved to New York City to study dance at the Alvin Ailey American Dance Center.

Is Jasmine Guy Married? Relationship

Guy married Terrence Duckett in August 1998, and the couple welcomed their first child, Imani, in 1999. Both divorced on April 8, 2008, after ten years of marriage. Guy and her child then moved to Atlanta, Guy’s childhood home. Guy voted for Senator Bernie Sanders in the 2016 presidential election in the United States.

How did Jasmine Guy start her Professional Career?

Career She made her television debut as a dancer in seven episodes of Fame in 1982. She then starred as Kayla in an episode of ‘The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air’ in 1991.

She played Halle Berry’s character’s mother in the 1993 TV mini-series ‘Queen,’ Dr. Jessica Bryce in ‘Living Single,’ Captain Teri Washington in ‘The Outer Limits,’ Delilah in ‘The Parkers,’ and Sawyer in ‘OK K.O! Let’s Be Heroes,’ and most recently Sawyer in ‘OK K.O! Let’s Be Heroes,’ in 2019.

Jasmine Guy also played Sawyer in ‘Cats Don’t Dance,’ Linda in ‘Guinevere,’ Tina in ‘Diamond Men,’ Janice in ‘Stomp the Yard: Homecoming,’ Nurse Mary in ‘October Baby,’ Leah Grimes in ‘Big Stone Gap,’ and other characters.

In addition to her acting career, she is a musician. Her self-titled debut album was released in 1990. Her album peaked at #143 on the US Top 200 Album Chart, with three singles: “Try Me,” “Another Like My Lover,” and “Just Want to Carry You.”
