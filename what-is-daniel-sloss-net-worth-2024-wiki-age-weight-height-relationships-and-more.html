Daniel Sloss Full Overview and Wiki

From Humble Beginnings to Netflix Stardom

Daniel Sloss’s journey from a small town in Scotland to international comedy stages is nothing short of remarkable.

His career kicked off at the tender age of 16, under the wing of comedian Frankie Boyle, which laid the foundation for his comedic path.

Sloss’s early exposure to the Edinburgh Festival Fringe, where he performed his first show, Life in 2D, marked the beginning of an upward trajectory in his career.

As Sloss matured, so did his comedy. By 19, he was the youngest comedian to perform a solo season in London’s West End.

His bold move to defer university education paid off as he continued to sell out shows, not just across the UK but around the globe.

His notable appearances on Conan and The Late Late Show with Craig Ferguson brought his Scottish charm to international audiences, further elevating his status.
